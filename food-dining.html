Food & Dining

Daal Baati Churma

  • Description: A quintessential Rajasthani dish consisting of Baati (baked wheat balls), Daal (spiced lentils), and Churma (a sweet mixture made of crushed Baati, ghee, and sugar).
  • Best Places to Eat:
    Chokhi Dhani: Offers a traditional experience of Daal Baati Churma served in a village-style setting with entertainment and cultural performances.
    Shree Mahavir Restaurant: Known for serving a hearty Daal Baati Churma.
    Thali House: Offers an extensive Rajasthani thali, which includes Daal Baati Churma among other dishes.
  • Cooking Workshops:
    Saffron Palate: Includes Daal Baati Churma as part of their traditional Rajasthani cooking workshops.
    Rajasthan Cooking School: Offers hands-on workshops where you can learn to prepare Daal Baati Churma from scratch.
  • Open Kitchens: Chokhi Dhani provides an open kitchen experience where you can watch the preparation of Daal Baati being baked in traditional clay ovens and served with Churma and Daal.

Ker Sangri

  • Description: A traditional Rajasthani dish made with ker berries (wild desert berries) and sangri beans, cooked with spices, and often served as a side dish.
  • Best Places to Eat:
    Chokhi Dhani: Offers an immersive experience with Rajasthani food, including Ker Sangri as part of the traditional Rajasthani thali.
    Laxmi Misthan Bhandar (LMB): Famous for its authentic Rajasthani food, including Ker Sangri served as part of a thali.
  • Cooking Workshops:
    Rajasthan Cooking School: Offers cooking classes where you can learn to make Ker Sangri and other Rajasthani specialties.
    Saffron Palate: This cooking school includes Ker Sangri in its traditional Rajasthani cuisine workshops.
  • Open Kitchens: At Chokhi Dhani, you can often watch the traditional preparation of Ker Sangri in a village-style open kitchen setup.

Laal Maans

  • Description: A fiery and spicy mutton curry made with red chilies, garlic, and Rajasthani spices, known for its bold flavors.
  • Best Places to Eat:
    Handi Restaurant: Famous for its rich, flavorful Laal Maans and other Rajasthani non-vegetarian dishes.
    Suvarna Mahal (Taj Rambagh Palace): Offers a royal dining experience with Laal Maans prepared in a traditional style.
    Sanjayâ€™s Dastarkhwan: A popular spot among locals for authentic Laal Maans.
  • Cooking Workshops:
    Rajasthan Cooking School: Offers workshops on making Laal Maans along with other local specialties.
    The Culinary Art School: Teaches traditional Rajasthani cooking, including Laal Maans.
  • Open Kitchens: Some fine dining restaurants like Suvarna Mahal (Taj Rambagh Palace) have an open kitchen or show kitchens where you can see chefs preparing traditional dishes like Laal Maans.

Ghewar

  • Description: A traditional Rajasthani sweet made from flour, ghee, and sugar syrup, often garnished with sliced almonds and saffron, popular during festivals.
  • Best Places to Eat:
    Laxmi Misthan Bhandar (LMB): Famous for Ghewar, especially during festivals like Teej and Diwali.
    Rawat Mishthan Bhandar: A popular spot to enjoy Ghewar and other sweets from Jaipur.
    Bikanerwala: Known for its delicious variety of Rajasthani sweets, including Ghewar.
  • Cooking Workshops:
    Rajasthan Cooking School: Offers specialized workshops on making traditional Rajasthani sweets like Ghewar.
    Bikanerwala: Occasionally holds cooking classes where you can learn to make Ghewar.
  • Open Kitchens: Laxmi Misthan Bhandar (LMB) has an open kitchen where you might see the process of Ghewar preparation, especially during busy festival times.
